# Article Frameworks

> Framework is skeleton. Craft is flesh. Insight is soul.
> A brilliant insight in a mediocre framework outperforms a hollow article in a perfect framework every time.
> But the right framework amplifies a good insight into an unforgettable one.

---

## Quick Selection Matrix

| Signal | Framework | Why | Danger Zone |
|--------|-----------|-----|-------------|
| Reader has a specific, recurring frustration | Pain-Point | Direct value, highest engagement baseline | Degrades into "5 tips" if pain isn't sharp enough |
| A real person + a genuine turning point | Story | Best completion rate, hardest to execute | Boring if the turn is predictable |
| 5+ genuinely useful, non-obvious items | Listicle | Most shareable, easiest to scan | Feels thin if items lack depth |
| Two choices smart people genuinely disagree on | Comparison | Drives comments and debate | Worthless if one option is obviously better |
| Breaking news / major shift (within 72h) | Hot Take | Highest initial CTR | Late or generic = noise, not signal |
| An uncomfortable truth nobody's naming | Exposé | Highest share rate when it lands | Needs bulletproof evidence |
| Uncertain | Pain-Point | Most forgiving framework | — |

## 1. Pain-Point (痛点型)

**Best for:** Reader has tried to solve a problem and keeps failing — or doesn't even realize why they're stuck.
**Rating:** Most reliable. Works for ~60% of topics.
**Emotional arc:** Recognition ("this is me") → Relief ("I'm not alone / not stupid") → Hope ("there's a way out") → Empowerment ("I can do this starting now")

### Opening — The Mirror (50–100 words)

Drop the reader into a moment they've LIVED. Not "很多人都有这个问题" — a scene so specific they flinch:

**Weak (generic, forgettable):**
> "很多职场人都面临工作效率低下的问题，每天忙忙碌碌却没有成果。"

**Strong (the reader IS the character):**
> "周三下午三点半。你已经开了四个会，每个会都有action item，没有一个你想动。桌上的美式凉了，微信有23条未读——不敢看，怕是新任务。你打开了今天的第一个工作文档。这时候你发现一件恐怖的事：你什么都没做，但你已经累了。"

**What separates good from great:** The mirror doesn't just describe the problem — it describes the *feeling* of the problem. The reader doesn't think "我也有这个问题", they think "他怎么知道的."

### Validation — "You're Not Alone" (100–200 words)

Prove this pain is widespread with HARD evidence:

- Specific numbers: "我在三个微信群做了个匿名投票，143个人里109个选了'每天至少有2小时在假装工作'"
- Named scenarios: "前两天有个做了6年运营的读者给我留言，说他每天最高效的时段是'等电梯的3分钟'，因为那3分钟他会疯狂回消息"
- Social proof with texture: Don't just say "很多人有同感." Quote a specific comment, DM, or overheard conversation

**DO NOT:** Cite vague statistics ("research shows..."), use "很多人/大部分人" without numbers, or make claims you can't source.

### Solution — The Ladder (400–800 words)

NOT a list of tips. A LADDER — each level builds on the previous.

**Critical execution:**

1. **Lead with the most counterintuitive solution** — earns trust immediately. "You'd expect me to say X. I'm going to say the opposite."
2. **Each solution must be ACTIONABLE TODAY** — "改变心态" is not a solution. "打开手机设置→屏幕使用时间→把微信的每日限额设为2小时" is a solution.
3. **Vary depth dramatically:** One solution gets 2 sentences. The next gets a full paragraph with a personal anecdote. The third gets 4 sentences. NEVER uniform depth.
4. **Connect levels:** "当你做到了上面这个，你会撞上一个新问题——" This creates narrative through the solutions, not a static list.
5. **Include one "ugly truth" solution** — the one nobody wants to hear. "说白了，最有效的方法也是最不sexy的那个：关掉通知。全部。包括微信。"

### Proof — Evidence It Works (100–200 words)

Before/after with specifics. Not "效果很好" — numbers, timeline, named outcomes:

> "用了两周之后最明显的变化不是效率——是焦虑感。以前每天晚上躺在床上会复盘今天又浪费了多少时间。现在不会了。不是因为突然变勤快了，是因为终于分清楚了'没产出'和'在恢复'的区别。具体数字：周报从平均拖3天变成了周五中午前交。OKR review从'没什么可说的'变成了能列出至少两个具体进展。"

### Close — One Punch (50 words max)

One sentence that reframes the whole article:

> "效率的真正敌人不是懒。是你一直在用意志力对抗一个本该用系统解决的问题。"

### Golden Quote Placement
- End of validation section (empathy peak)
- After the most counterintuitive solution (insight peak)

## 2. Story (故事型)

**Best for:** A real person with a real turning point and a transferable insight.
**Rating:** Highest completion rate when executed well. Hardest to execute — requires genuine narrative surprise.
**Emotional arc:** Curiosity ("who is this?") → Investment ("I care what happens") → Tension ("what will they do?") → Surprise ("I didn't see that coming") → Reflection ("what does this mean for me?")

### Hook — The Flash-Forward (30–50 words)

Start at the most dramatic or paradoxical moment. The reader must NEED to know what happened:

**Weak (spoils the story):**
> "今天给大家讲一个创业者的故事，他经历了很多困难，最后成功了。"

**Strong (creates an irresistible question):**
> "2019年的最后一天，他把刚拿到的800万融资全部退了回去。投资人以为他疯了。他后来说：'那是我做过的最理智的决定，虽然当时我的合伙人差点跟我动手。'"

**What separates good from great:** The hook creates a SPECIFIC question ("why did he return the money?") that the reader physically cannot scroll past without answering.

### Context — Building Stakes (150–300 words)

Make the reader care about this person BEFORE the turning point:

- Show constraints that make the situation seem impossible — "公司账上只够发两个月工资"
- Build tension through specific details: "所有人都在等他拍板。会议室里安静到能听见空调的嗡嗡声。"
- Give the protagonist ONE recognizable human quality — not "he was brave," but show a moment of fear, hesitation, or stubbornness

**NEVER info-dump background.** Weave context through the story. The reader learns about the person by watching them act, not by reading their LinkedIn profile.

### Turn — The Moment (200–400 words)

This is the climax. SLOW THE PACING:

- Sensory detail: What did the room look like? What was the weather? What was on the desk?
- Internal conflict: Show the character wrestling, not just deciding. "他点了根烟——已经戒了三年了"
- The decision must feel EARNED — not lucky, not obvious in hindsight
- **Critical: The reader should NOT be able to predict the turn from the hook.** If they can, the story has no surprise.

> "他在那个会议室坐了整整四个小时。手机震了27次——都是催他签字的。最后让他做决定的不是什么商业分析。是他女儿发来的一条语音：'爸爸你今天还加班吗？我给你留了块蛋糕。'他听了三遍。然后拿起笔——划掉了自己的名字。"

### Insight — What This Means (200–300 words)

Extract the transferable lesson WITHOUT moralizing:

- Frame as "这件事让我想了很久的是..." not "这个故事告诉我们..."
- The insight must SURPRISE — if predictable from the hook, dig deeper
- Bridge to the reader: "你大概率不会遇到退800万融资的决定。但你一定遇到过这种时刻——所有的理性分析都指向A，但你的直觉在说B。"

### Close — The Echo (50–100 words)

Return to a detail from earlier, but with new meaning:

> "后来有人问他当时怎么想的。他说：'我没在想。我在听。那个蛋糕的语音我存了，到现在还在。'——这句话不是关于蛋糕。是关于你在最吵的时候，还能不能听见最轻的那个声音。"

### Golden Quote Placement
- Maximum tension moment before the turn
- The single most transferable insight sentence

## 3. Listicle (清单型)

**Best for:** Methods, tools, or techniques where you have 5+ genuinely valuable, non-obvious items.
**Rating:** Easiest to share. Highest risk of feeling like Wikipedia.
**Emotional arc:** Promise ("this will be worth it") → Escalating value (each item surprises more than the last) → "How did I not know this?" peak → Gratitude + immediate action

### Promise — The Contract (50–80 words)

Be specific about what you're delivering AND why the reader should trust you:

**Weak:**
> "今天分享一些好用的工具，希望对大家有帮助。"

**Strong:**
> "以下7个工具，是我花了14个月、测了差不多40多个产品、踩了无数坑之后留下来的。标准很简单：免费或极便宜，不需要教程就能上手，用了之后你会生气——气自己为什么不早知道。每个我都附了真实使用场景和最容易掉进去的坑。"

**What separates good from great:** The promise is specific enough that the reader can JUDGE if you kept it. "一些好用的工具" can never be falsified. "14个月测了40多个" can.

### Items — Deep, Not Wide (800–1,500 words total)

Each item needs ALL of these:

1. **What it is** (1 sentence max — name and core function)
2. **Specific scenario where it shines** — not "improves productivity," but "当你需要在30分钟内把10页PDF变成一个不丢人的PPT的时候"
3. **Your real experience** — a specific moment when it saved you or surprised you
4. **The catch** — every tool has one. Naming it proves you actually used it and builds trust

**CRITICAL: Vary the depth DRAMATICALLY.**

- Item 1: 200 words (your star pick, with a real story)
- Item 2: 60 words (quick but punchy)
- Item 3: 150 words (with a twist or gotcha)
- Item 4: 40 words (this one speaks for itself)
- Item 5: 180 words (the sleeper hit nobody expects)

Uniform depth = reader's pattern-recognition kicks in = boredom = exit.

**DO NOT:** Just describe each item. You must show you've USED it and have an OPINION. "This tool is good" = worthless. "This tool saved my ass at 2am when a client needed revisions in 3 hours" = valuable.

### Star Pick + Bonus (100–150 words)

> "如果你只试一个，选#3。原因很简单：学习成本最低，效果最容易感知，你今天就能用上。另外有一个没放进正式名单的——因为它适合的人群很窄，但如果你刚好是那个人，它值一整篇文章。"

### Golden Quote Placement
- One punchy summary per item (quotable and shareable)
- The star pick rationale

## 4. Comparison (对比型)

**Best for:** Two options that smart people genuinely disagree on. A real choice with real trade-offs.
**Rating:** Strongest comment driver. Requires genuine balance.
**Emotional arc:** "I've been wondering this" → Investment in both sides → Increasing clarity → "I know which one is mine now"

### The Dilemma (50–100 words)

Frame as a decision the reader is CURRENTLY making. And if possible, REFRAME the question:

**Weak (textbook intro):**
> "Python和JavaScript是两种流行的编程语言，各有优缺点。"

**Strong (reframes the question entirely):**
> "最近半年，我被问得最多的问题就是'该学Python还是JavaScript'。但这两年带了几十个新人之后，我发现一个事：这个问题本身就是错的。你不是在选语言。你是在选你想成为哪种开发者——而这两种人的职业轨迹，在第三年之后会走向完全不同的地方。"

**What separates good from great:** Reframing the question itself is the highest-value move. If you change HOW the reader thinks about the choice, you've already won.

### Deep Dive A & B (300–500 words each)

For EACH option:

- Strengths with named evidence (not "it's good at X" — show evidence)
- Weaknesses that only someone with experience would know — the kind you discover after 6 months, not from reading docs
- The ideal person profile: "If you are [specific person] in [specific situation], this is your answer. Period."

**BOTH options must feel genuinely viable.** If B is clearly weaker, you don't have a comparison — you have a recommendation disguised as balance. Switch frameworks.

### The Table

| Dimension | Option A | Option B |

Visual, scannable, max 6 rows. Use SPECIFIC descriptors — "学习曲线陡但3个月后起飞" not "difficult."

### Your Take (100–150 words)

State your actual position with a PERSONAL reason:

> "如果是现在的我重新选，我选Python。原因只有一个：我现在做的事情，90%是数据相关的。但如果两年前问我？JS，毫不犹豫——因为当时我需要的是最快做出能用的东西。所以真正的问题不是哪个好，是你未来两年想做什么。"

**NEVER end with "it depends on your situation"** — that's a non-answer dressed as wisdom.

### Golden Quote Placement
- The reframe of the original question
- Your verdict sentence

## 5. Hot Take (热点解读型)

**Best for:** Breaking news, industry shifts, policy changes within 72 hours.
**Rating:** Highest initial CTR. Shortest shelf life.
**Emotional arc:** "Wait, what happened?" → "Here's what everyone thinks" → "But here's what they're missing" → "Here's what this means for you" → "Here's what to do now"

### News Flash (50–100 words)

What happened, stripped to facts. No opinion yet. But tease that you see something others don't:

> "昨天凌晨，OpenAI发了一篇博客，宣布GPT-5。一小时内全网刷屏，讨论量破亿。但在所有人都在讨论'它多强'的时候，我注意到了博客里一句被所有人忽略的话。"

### Surface Layer — What Everyone Sees (100–200 words)

The mainstream narrative, presented fairly and concisely. Prove you understand the common view BEFORE challenging it. This earns the right to disagree.

### Deep Layer — What You See (300–500 words)

This is your VALUE. The insight that justifies the article's existence.

**Requirements:**

- At least ONE piece of information or perspective not in mainstream coverage
- Specific evidence or reasoning — not just assertion
- Frame as: "但如果你把时间线拉长...", "有个细节被所有人忽略了...", "这让我想到一个更大的问题..."
- **The "Why Now?" test:** Why is your take worth reading TODAY? If it could have been written a week ago about a different event, it's too generic.

### Impact Forecast (200–400 words)

3-5 specific, testable predictions:

- Each must be concrete enough that you could be proven wrong (= valuable)
- Order from "affects you this week" → "affects you this year"
- For each: explain the MECHANISM, not just the prediction

> - **本周影响：** 你收件箱里会出现至少3封"我们已接入GPT-5"的营销邮件。别点。等两周看真实测评。
> - **三个月内：** 现在月薪1.5万的AI提示词工程师岗位会消失一半。不是因为不需要了——是因为GPT-5让提示词变得没那么重要了。
> - **一年内：** 真正的战场不在模型本身，而在谁能把模型塞进现有工作流里、而不被用户骂。

### Action Items (50–100 words)

Concrete. Immediate. Doable today:

> "今天你可以做一件事：去试用GPT-5的免费版，但不要测'写作文'这种无聊需求。拿你手上最棘手的一个真实工作任务喂给它。看它能做到什么程度。这比读100篇分析文章都有用。"

### Golden Quote Placement
- Core deep-layer insight
- Boldest impact prediction

## 6. Exposé (真相型)

**Best for:** An uncomfortable truth that everyone avoids naming. The "emperor has no clothes" moment.
**Rating:** Highest share rate when it lands. Requires courage and evidence.
**Emotional arc:** Discomfort → "I knew something was off" → Evidence pile-up → "I can't unsee this" → Clarity (even if uncomfortable)

### The Reveal (50–100 words)

Name the uncomfortable truth upfront. Don't build to it — lead with it:

> "说一个不讨好的事实：90%的知识付费课程，卖的不是知识，是焦虑的临时缓解。你买课的那一刻感觉自己在进步——那个感觉就是产品本身。学没学到东西，是另一回事。"

**What separates good from great:** The reveal must be genuinely uncomfortable — not something everyone already agrees with. If readers nod without flinching, it's not an exposé, it's validation.

### The Evidence (400–600 words)

This is where the article lives or dies. Stack evidence methodically:

- Personal observation with specifics
- Data that supports the claim
- Named examples (anonymized if necessary)
- The mechanism: WHY this happens (not just THAT it happens)

**Critical fairness requirement:** Acknowledge the legitimate reasons why the situation exists. "这不是说所有知识付费都是骗局。但你需要学会分辨哪些在教你东西，哪些在卖你感觉。" An unfair exposé is a hit piece. A fair one is an act of respect toward the reader's intelligence.

### The Reframe (100–200 words)

Give the reader a new lens to evaluate what you've exposed:

> "所以下次你想买课的时候，问自己一个问题：如果这个课程免费，你还会学吗？如果答案是'不会'——你不是在为知识付费，你在为逃避真正的工作付费。"

### Close — The Dare (50 words)

Challenge the reader to act differently:

> "你下一个想买的课程，先放24小时。如果24小时后你还想买——买。如果你已经忘了——恭喜，你刚省了299块和一个永远不会打开的PDF。"

### Golden Quote Placement
- The core reveal statement
- The reframe question

## Depth Requirements (ALL Frameworks)

Structure alone is never enough. Before any framework is "executed," verify:

1. **The Dinner Test:** If the reader told a friend about this article over dinner, what would they say? If the answer is a vague "讲AI的，写得不错" — the article has no sharp insight to carry.
2. **The Google Test:** Search the topic. Read top 3 results. Does your article say ANYTHING new? Better-written repetition is still repetition.
3. **The Specificity Count:** Count concrete examples (real numbers, named scenarios, specific experiences). Fewer than 3 in 2000 words = too abstract.
4. **The Counterargument Test:** Have you included the STRONGEST objection — not a strawman — and addressed it?
5. **The Screenshot Test:** Is there at least one paragraph someone would screenshot and send to a friend?

## Framework Combinations

For complex topics, combine two frameworks. Use ONE for overall structure, embed elements of the other:

| Combination | How It Works | Best For |
|-------------|-------------|----------|
| Story + Pain-Point | Story hook → protagonist's pain → solutions that emerged | Making advice feel earned and real |
| Hot Take + Comparison | News breaks → compare two response strategies | Helping readers navigate breaking events |
| Pain-Point + Listicle | Frame the pain → deliver solutions as curated list | When the problem is clear and tools exist |
| Story + Hot Take | One person's experience illustrates a broader shift | Humanizing abstract trends |
| Exposé + Pain-Point | Name the uncomfortable truth → show a better path | When the reader is currently being harmed by the status quo |
| Comparison + Story | Frame the choice → tell the story of someone who chose each path | Making abstract decisions concrete |

## Framework Selection Gotchas

**"Pain-Point default":** If you're using Pain-Point for every article, content becomes predictable. Force Story or Exposé at least once every 3 articles.

**"The story without a turn":** If your story has no genuine surprise, switch to Pain-Point. A flat story is worse than no story.

**"The fake comparison":** If one option is obviously better, you have a recommendation, not a comparison. Either find genuine strengths in the "weaker" option or switch frameworks.

**"The stale hot take":** News >48 hours old without a genuinely unique angle = noise. Don't write it. A late generic hot take actively damages credibility.

**"The toothless exposé":** If your "uncomfortable truth" is something everyone already agrees with, it's not an exposé — it's validation dressed as courage. The truth must make at least some readers flinch.
