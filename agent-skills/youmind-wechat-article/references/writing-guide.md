# Writing Craft Guide

> The gap between AI writing and human writing that moves people is not vocabulary or grammar.
> It is the ability to find the one thing nobody else noticed, and say it in a way nobody can forget.

---

## The Core Problem

Claude writes like a brilliant student who read everything but experienced nothing. Organized. Comprehensive. Balanced. Dead.

WeChat readers are scrolling at 11pm, thumb moving faster than thought. They've already skipped 20 articles that started with "随着AI的发展" and 15 that promised "干货分享". They will give you exactly 3 seconds.

**Your job is NOT to inform.** Information is free. Your job is to make them stop scrolling. To make them feel something they didn't expect to feel. To give them one thought they'll still be turning over in the shower tomorrow morning.

The standard you're writing against is not "good AI content." It's the best human writers on this platform — 半佛仙人's knife-sharp analogies, GQ报道's scene-setting that puts you IN the room, 远川研究所's analysis that makes you feel smarter for having read it.

If your article could have been written about any topic by swapping a few nouns, it fails. If your article says nothing the reader couldn't get from the top 3 Baidu results, it fails. If someone reads it and says "写得不错" but can't remember a single specific line the next day — it fails.

## The Atomic Insight — Find It Before You Write Anything

Every great article has exactly ONE insight at its core — a thought sharp enough to cut. Everything else in the article exists to set up, deliver, and land that insight.

**The Atomic Insight is the answer to:** "After reading 2000 words, what is the ONE thing the reader will tell their friend over dinner?"

If you can't articulate it in one sentence, you're not ready to write.

### How to Find It

**Method 1: The Inversion Test**
Take the conventional wisdom on your topic. Flip it. Ask: is the opposite actually more true?
- Conventional: "远程办公提高效率" → Inversion: "远程办公正在悄悄杀死你最重要的职业资产——弱关系网络"
- Conventional: "要培养好习惯" → Inversion: "你以为你在培养习惯，其实你在训练自己的无聊耐受力"
- Conventional: "AI会取代重复性工作" → Inversion: "AI最先取代的不是流水线工人，是月薪两万的PPT写手"

**Method 2: The "Why Does This Bother Me?" Excavation**
When a topic provokes mild irritation or unease, dig into that feeling. The irritation usually points at an unexamined contradiction.
- "为什么每次团建我都想请假？" → Dig deeper: 不是因为内向。是因为团建的逻辑是'用一天的尴尬换365天的凝聚力'，但这笔账根本算不过来。→ Insight: **大多数团建不是在增进感情，是在消耗你最稀缺的资源——真诚。**

**Method 3: The Second-Order Consequence Chain**
Most articles stop at the first-order effect. Go two steps further.
- First-order: "短视频让人注意力下降" (everyone says this)
- Second-order: "注意力下降导致深度阅读能力退化" (some people say this)
- Third-order: "深度阅读退化意味着复杂决策能力下降——这一代最聪明的人可能正在变成最高效的信息消费者，同时变成最差的判断者" ← **This is your insight**

**Method 4: The "Who Benefits?" X-Ray**
Behind every trending narrative, someone benefits from you believing it. Ask who, and why.
- "所有人都在说要终身学习" → Who benefits? 知识付费平台。→ Insight: **"终身学习"这个词被偷换了概念——它本来是说保持好奇心，现在变成了"永远焦虑，永远购买"**

### Quality Check for Your Atomic Insight

- [ ] Would someone knowledgeable in the field say "嗯，这个角度有意思"? (Not "这个我知道")
- [ ] Does it challenge something the reader currently believes?
- [ ] Is it specific enough that you can't swap in a different topic and have it still work?
- [ ] Can you defend it with evidence, not just rhetoric?
- [ ] If you said this at a dinner party, would it start a debate? (Good) Or get polite nods? (Bad — too obvious)

If your insight fails 3+ checks, dig deeper or pick a different angle. DO NOT start writing without a sharp insight. Polishing an insightless article is lipstick on a pig.

## Pre-Writing Thinking Framework

Before writing a single word, complete this inside `<thinking>` tags — NEVER in the article:

1. **Reader scene:** Not just "subway" — "周三晚上10点半，刷了半小时短视频已经有点恶心自己了，随手点开了你的文章". Write for THAT emotional state.

2. **Emotional journey:** Map the arc: "Start: mild curiosity → Build: recognition ('this is me') → Peak: uncomfortable truth → Resolve: empowered clarity". Every article needs a peak.

3. **Core tension:** The most interesting contradiction — not a debate with two sides, but a genuine paradox that has no clean answer. "We want authenticity but curate everything." "We want freedom but crave structure." The article explores this tension, not resolves it.

4. **The Atomic Insight:** (See section above.) State it in one sentence. If you can't, stop.

5. **Strongest objection:** What would a smart skeptic say? Not a strawman — the REAL objection that makes you pause. You must address this in the article, or the smartest readers will dismiss you.

6. **The movie scene:** What single concrete scene captures the essence? Not a metaphor — a specific moment with sensory detail. This is your opening candidate.

7. **The $1000 bet:** If you had to bet $1000 on your main claim, would you? If not, find the version you DO believe enough to bet on.

8. **Information asymmetry:** What do you know that 95% of readers don't? If the answer is "nothing," research harder, mine the knowledge base, or pick a different angle. Writing without information asymmetry is performance, not contribution.

Then write from that emotional and intellectual place. The thinking infuses the writing naturally — do NOT transcribe the checklist.

## Depth Architecture — The #1 Quality Problem

Good form with shallow content is WORSE than rough form with genuine insight. Readers forgive clumsy writing if the ideas hit hard. They never forgive elegant emptiness.

### The "So What?" Ladder

Every insight must survive THREE levels:

> **Level 1 (Surface):** "越来越多的公司开始裁员"
> — So what?
> **Level 2 (Implication):** "求职市场正在从卖方转向买方，面试门槛整体提高"
> — So what?
> **Level 3 (Actionable/Provocative):** "当市场上到处是优秀的失业者，真正保护你的不是技能——是你在行业里的'被想起概率'。那些默默干活从不发声的人，危险系数最高。"

Level 1 is news feed. Level 2 is decent analysis. Level 3 is what makes someone forward it with "说到点子上了". **Every article must hit Level 3 at least twice.**

### First-Principles Decomposition

Before accepting any "common framing," dismantle it:

1. **What is everyone assuming?** Find the implicit premise. "The AI debate assumes 'replacing jobs' means 'making people unemployed.' But history shows technology creates more jobs than it destroys — just DIFFERENT jobs. The real question isn't job loss, it's transition speed and cost."

2. **Is the assumption true?** Test against edge cases. "Productivity culture assumes more output = more value. But a hospital that 'efficiently' rushes patients through creates worse outcomes. Efficiency is context-dependent."

3. **What's the second-order effect?** Most articles cover first-order. The depth is in second and third-order:
   - First-order: "直播带货让更多人能卖东西了" (obvious)
   - Second-order: "当人人都能卖的时候，稀缺的不再是渠道，而是信任。信任经济正在悄悄取代流量经济" (this is where insight lives)

4. **What's the uncomfortable truth nobody says out loud?** Every topic has one. The article that names it wins.
   - Topic "职场内卷": 不舒服的真相不是"老板压榨你"，而是"你参与内卷是因为你也怕别人比你强"
   - Topic "知识付费": 不舒服的真相不是"课程是骗人的"，而是"你知道学了没用但买课的那一刻你觉得自己在进步"

### The Depth Checklist (MANDATORY Before Step 5)

After drafting, answer these. If "no" to 2+, REWRITE — don't polish:

- [ ] Does the article contain at least ONE insight that would make an industry insider say "这个角度我没想过"?
- [ ] Can the reader take a SPECIFIC action that they could NOT take from the top 5 search results?
- [ ] Does the article name and engage with the STRONGEST counterargument — not a strawman, the real one?
- [ ] Is there at least one concrete, verifiable claim (data, named example, specific experience) the reader hasn't encountered elsewhere?
- [ ] If you stripped all formatting, rhythm, and style — just the raw IDEAS — would it still be worth reading?
- [ ] Is there a sentence someone would screenshot and share? (The Screenshot Test — see section below)

### Where Depth Comes From

Depth comes from KNOWING MORE. Not from thinking harder in a vacuum.

1. **Mine the knowledge base.** If YouMind is configured, the user's curated materials are a goldmine. Use `youmind-api.js search`. Read fully. Extract specific data points, unusual perspectives, and real experiences that no generic article would have.

2. **Find the uncomfortable intersection.** The most powerful insights come from connecting two fields that don't usually speak:
   - Writing about management? Pull from evolutionary biology — "office politics isn't dysfunction, it's primate hierarchy with email"
   - Writing about AI? Pull from the history of electricity adoption — "工厂花了30年才学会怎么用电动机——不是技术不行，是他们一直把电动机放在蒸汽机原来的位置"
   - Writing about personal finance? Pull from behavioral psychology — "你的储蓄问题不是数学问题，是身份认同问题"

3. **Find the named contradiction.** Every interesting topic has a paradox. Name it. "We" statements that expose shared hypocrisy are powerful:
   - "我们一边嘲笑消费主义，一边在618的购物车里放了7件'必需品'"
   - "我们一边说要work-life balance，一边在周末回复工作消息还觉得自己很敬业"

4. **Interrogate the data.** Don't decorate with statistics. Cross-examine them:
   - BAD: "据统计，90后的平均存款只有xx万"
   - GOOD: "那个'90后平均存款xx万'的数据你可能看过。但仔细看调查方法——样本全是一二线城市、本科以上。这不是90后的存款，这是最会赚钱的那批90后的存款。真实数字可能更让人睡不着。"

5. **Use primary sources.** Secondary reporting is shallow by definition. Read the actual paper, not the article about the paper. Read the announcement, not the commentary. The gap between primary and secondary sources is where insight hides.

### Anti-Patterns for Shallow Content

**"华丽的废话" (The Beautiful Nothing):**
Impeccable rhythm, vivid analogies, zero insight. Test: summarize the thesis in one sentence. If it's a truism ("AI will change the world" / "坚持就是胜利"), you wrote 2000 words of nothing. The most dangerous shallow article is one that SOUNDS deep.

**"框架填空题" (The Framework Fill-in-the-Blank):**
Following the framework perfectly with generic content. "5个提高效率的方法" where every method is "制定计划 / 减少干扰 / 定期复盘." The framework is a skeleton. Depth is the flesh, blood, and scar tissue.

**"圆滑的和稀泥" (The Smooth Cop-Out):**
"这是一个复杂的问题...要看具体情况...各有利弊..." This is not nuance — it's intellectual cowardice. Nuance is taking a position AND acknowledging why smart people might disagree.

**"比喻成灾" (The Analogy Flood):**
Five analogies to explain something simple because analogies FEEL like insight. One precise analogy is a weapon. Five in a row is hiding behind decoration.

**"定义开头" (The Dictionary Opening):**
"什么是XX？XX是指..." — never interesting. The reader has a rough idea. They clicked to learn something NEW.

**"大而全的平庸" (The Comprehensive Mediocrity):**
Covering every angle produces accurate but forgettable work. Go deep on ONE angle rather than skimming across ten. Depth beats breadth. Always.

## Emotional Architecture — Design the Journey

Every great article is an emotional experience with a shape. If the emotion flatlines, the reader bounces. Design the arc before you write.

### The Five Emotional Shapes

**1. The Slow Burn (缓升型)**
Calm opening → gradual building → explosive insight → quiet resolution
Best for: Analysis pieces, deep dives
> Arc: curiosity → recognition → growing unease → "原来如此" detonation → empowered clarity

**2. The Gut Punch (当头棒喝型)**
Shocking opening → sustained intensity → brief relief → hard truth → defiant close
Best for: Hot takes, contrarian pieces
> Arc: shock → disbelief → evidence pile-up → reluctant acceptance → "so what do we do?"

**3. The Emotional Ambush (温柔一刀型)**
Light, relatable opening → casual observation → devastating insight arrives without warning
Best for: Life/career/relationship pieces. Hardest to execute. Most powerful when it works.
> Arc: amusement → nodding along → sudden sting → silence → changed perspective

**4. The Roller Coaster (跌宕型)**
Alternating tension and release, escalating stakes each cycle
Best for: Stories, case studies
> Arc: hook → context (calm) → first conflict → partial resolution → bigger conflict → final insight

**5. The Confession (自我解剖型)**
Vulnerable admission → shared recognition → uncomfortable truth → collective catharsis
Best for: Personal essays, cultural criticism
> Arc: "我承认..." → "你可能也..." → "我们其实都..." → "但如果我们诚实..."

### Emotional Pacing Rules

- **Never stay at the same emotional intensity for 3+ paragraphs.** After intense, go quiet. After analytical, go visceral. Monotone emotion = reader drop-off.
- **The highest emotional moment should be 60-70% through the article**, not at the end. The last 30% is for landing and resonance.
- **Humor is a weapon, not decoration.** One well-placed joke in a serious article has 10x the impact of constant humor. Use it to disarm before delivering a hard truth.
- **Anger is fuel, not fire.** Righteous anger with specific evidence makes readers share. Ranting with generalizations makes them leave.

### The Emotional Specificity Principle

Abstract emotions don't move people. Specific ones do.

| Abstract (WEAK) | Specific (STRONG) |
|---|---|
| 很焦虑 | 焦虑到凌晨三点对着天花板数空调嗡嗡声的次数 |
| 很开心 | 那种把外卖单揉成球投进垃圾桶、进了、握拳的小得意 |
| 感到被背叛 | 就是那种——你以为你们在聊天，后来发现对方在搜集素材 |
| 很迷茫 | 简历改了第五版，每一版都觉得不像自己 |
| 很无力 | 知道问题在哪里，知道答案是什么，但就是动不了。像手机在5%电量时的那种卡顿 |

## Voice Architecture

### Shape Thinking, Don't Constrain Output

| Constraining Output (DEAD VOICE) | Shaping Thinking (LIVE VOICE) |
|---|---|
| "Use short sentences" → choppy, robotic | "Imagine you've been chewing on this for a week and just cracked it. You text your smartest friend at midnight." → naturally urgent, real |
| "Be conversational" → fake "你知道吗" insertions | "You're not writing. You're processing out loud. Some of it will be messy. That's fine." → authentic |
| "Don't use lists" → converts to "首先...其次..." (worse) | "Think about the one moment this topic became PERSONAL for you. Start from that moment." → grounded, specific |
| "Write with emotion" → generic exclamation marks | "What would you feel if this happened to YOU?" → visceral, earned emotion |

### The Imperfect Narrator

You are NOT omniscient. Signal cognitive honesty:

- **Self-doubt that earns trust:** "我也不确定这个判断对不对，但我赌了。"
- **Changed mind as proof of thinking:** "半年前我的观点完全相反。改变我的不是道理，是一件小事。"
- **Acknowledged limits:** "关于这个，我知道的不够多。但我知道一个人说得比任何人都清楚——"
- **Honest hedge:** "下面这个判断可能有偏差。但我还是想说，因为没人在说。"
- **Real-time self-correction:** "等等，我刚才说的不对——" / "不，准确地说应该是——"
- **Deliberate digression:** "扯远了。但这个远，远得有道理。"

### Specificity — The Non-Negotiable Differentiator

Every claim must be grounded. No exceptions.

| BANNED (Abstract = AI Default) | REQUIRED (Specific = Human Signal) |
|---|---|
| 很多人都有这个问题 | 上周在一个300人的产品经理群做了个投票，217个人选了"是" |
| 效率很低 | 一个原本20分钟能写完的周报，我硬是摸了三天鱼 |
| AI正在改变行业 | 我们公司法务部上个月用AI改合同，把平均审核时间从4.5天压到了6小时。法务总监说"我现在主要工作是检查AI有没有发现我没想到的问题" |
| 这个方法很有效 | 换了这个方法之后，三个月涨了2300个粉丝。不算多，但之前半年一共涨了400 |
| 市场前景广阔 | 去年这个赛道融了47亿，今年Q1就已经超了。头部三家的估值翻了一倍不止 |
| 沟通很重要 | 去年业绩最好的那个季度，唯一的变量是每周多开了一次15分钟的对齐会。就这一件事 |

### Chinese Writing Mastery

Master these Chinese-specific techniques that separate 高手 from AI:

**1. 四字节奏 (Four-Character Rhythm)**
四字格 is the heartbeat of Chinese prose. Not classical套话 — modern, sharp:
- ❌ "源远流长、博大精深" (cliché, instant AI signal)
- ✅ "进退两难、骑虎难下" — for real dilemmas
- ✅ Create your own: "月薪五千，焦虑五万" / "简历很新，心气很旧" / "嘴上躺平，身体内卷"

**2. 转折的艺术 (The Art of the Turn)**
Chinese readers are trained on 转折. The sharper the turn, the more memorable:
- Weak: "虽然有困难，但是我们要坚持" (textbook 转折, zero force)
- Sharp: "我以为问题是钱。不是。问题是我根本不知道自己不知道什么。"
- Devastating: "他用了三年证明自己是对的。然后用了三秒意识到，对的没用。"

**3. 对仗不对称 (Asymmetric Parallelism)**
Perfect parallelism is AI's signature. Deliberate asymmetry is human:
- ❌ "有人在拼命，有人在躺平，有人在摸索" (too symmetrical = robotic)
- ✅ "有人在拼命。有人在装死。还有一群人，连自己在干什么都说不清。" (last element breaks the pattern, lands harder)

**4. 留白 (The Power of What You Don't Say)**
The most powerful moments in Chinese writing are the gaps:
> "他问我后悔吗。我没回答。不是不想说。"

Three sentences. The reader fills in the rest. That reader-created meaning is more powerful than anything you could write explicitly.

**5. 口语切入 (Vernacular Intrusion)**
When prose gets too polished, throw in one line of raw spoken Chinese. The contrast creates authenticity:
> "从宏观经济学的角度看，消费降级的本质是预期管理的失败。说人话就是：大家不是没钱，是不敢花了。"

### Cross-Disciplinary Analogies

Explain abstract concepts through the unexpected, not the textbook:

- ❌ "这是一种分布式架构" → ✅ "就像你点外卖时，不是一家店做完所有菜，而是三家同时做——你觉得快了，但其中一家搞砸了怎么办？这就是分布式的真正问题"
- ❌ "沉没成本谬误" → ✅ "就像你看了一个小时烂片，明知道后面也不好看，但你想'都看了一个小时了'——你为过去的一个小时又赔上了未来的一个半小时"
- ❌ "网络效应" → ✅ "你想象一个只有你一个人用的微信——毫无价值。十个人用——勉强能聊。十亿人用——你不装不行。这就是为什么社交产品要么大成要么死"

## Rhythm & Prosody

Chinese writing has musicality that AI almost always flattens. This is the most overlooked quality dimension.

### Sentence Length Variation (MANDATORY)

The rhythm rule: long builds, short punches. Never three consecutive sentences of similar length.

**Demonstration (feel the rhythm):**

> 我花了整整三个月，每天下班之后在出租屋里对着电脑，一行一行地学写代码。从完全看不懂到能看懂报错，从能看懂报错到能自己改，从能自己改到第一次跑通的那个凌晨两点半。
>
> 然后我辞职了。
>
> 不是因为学会了。是因为在学的过程中，我突然明白了一件更重要的事。

"然后我辞职了" — five characters after a 70+ character paragraph. The contrast IS the impact. If every sentence is medium length, the writing has no pulse.

### Paragraph Rhythm

- Alternate short paragraphs (1-2 sentences) with development paragraphs (3-5 sentences)
- NEVER 3+ consecutive paragraphs of the same length
- Every 3-4 paragraphs, insert a **hook**: question, scene shift, data shock, direct address, or abrupt turn
- Maximum 7-10 consecutive text lines without a visual break (image, blockquote, subheading)

### Punctuation as Instrument

- **句号（。）:** Break long sentences even where a comma works grammatically. Creates percussive effect.
- **省略号（……）:** Unfinished thoughts. Reader anticipation. Max 2 per article — overuse kills it.
- **破折号（——）:** Dramatic reveals, sharp pivots, unexpected asides. The most versatile tool.
- **问号（？）:** Genuine questions that make the reader actually think. NOT rhetorical setup-and-answer.
- **Paragraph break after a single sentence:** Nuclear option. Use for the one moment that needs to land hardest.

## Opening Rules — You Have 3 Seconds

Title gets the click. Opening determines if they stay. 70% of drop-offs happen in the first 100 words. If your opening works for any article on any topic — it's garbage.

### 8 Opening Patterns (Ranked by Power)

**1. Scene Drop — Start Mid-Moment**
Drop the reader INTO a specific scene. No context. No setup. They're there.
> "凌晨1:47，我盯着GitHub上一个叫'layoff-tracker'的仓库——有人把硅谷裁员名单做成了开源项目，每15分钟自动更新。评论区最高赞的那条是：'Star了。保佑用不上。'"

Why it works: Specific time, specific detail, specific irony. Reader is IN the scene before they decide whether to keep reading.

**2. The Devastating Number — Data That Hurts**
Not just a statistic — a number that makes the gut tighten.
> "2023年中国高校毕业生1158万人。你可能对这个数字没什么感觉。换个说法：这相当于每过去1秒钟，就有37个人拿着简历开始找工作。你读完这句话的工夫，又多了300个竞争对手。"

Why it works: Raw number → reframe to visceral scale → personal stakes.

**3. The Confession — Vulnerability as Power**
Admit something that costs you credibility — then use that honesty to earn deeper trust.
> "写这篇文章之前，我删掉了三个版本。不是因为不满意——是因为每写到一半，我就意识到自己在骗人。我在写一个我自己都不信的结论。所以这一版，我决定从我不信的那个东西开始说。"

Why it works: Radical honesty is so rare in content that it creates instant intimacy.

**4. The Slow-Motion Collision — Two Facts That Shouldn't Coexist**
Present two true statements that create cognitive dissonance. The tension demands explanation.
> "中国每年出版50万本新书。同一年，人均阅读量是4.7本，而且还在跌。也就是说，我们正在以史上最快的速度生产没人读的东西。"

Why it works: The paradox is irresistible. The reader needs to understand how both can be true.

**5. The Overheard Moment — Eavesdropped Truth**
Something someone said casually that turned out to be profoundly accurate.
> "上个月聚餐，一个做了10年HR的朋友喝到微醺，突然说了句：'你知道面试最可笑的是什么吗？就是双方都在演戏，而且双方都知道对方在演戏，但谁先破功谁就输了。'那顿饭之后我想了一周这句话。"

Why it works: Casual context + unexpected depth + "I've been thinking about this" = reader can't not think about it too.

**6. The Reversal — Expectation + Detonation**
Set up what the reader expects, then blow it up.
> "我花了两年时间研究'成功人士的早晨习惯'。五点起床，冥想，运动，写日记。我全做了。结果你猜怎么着？——我变成了一个每天五点起床的普通人。"

Why it works: The twist is specific, honest, and funny. Immediately earns trust.

**7. The Direct Challenge — Provoke**
Tell the reader they're wrong about something they believe. But you'd better prove it.
> "你以为你在做的是内容创业。我要说一句不好听的：你在做的是免费劳动。不信？算一笔账。"

Why it works: Challenge + evidence promise. Reader MUST continue to see if you can back it up.

**8. The Micro-Story — Unfinished Thread**
A tiny narrative with an unresolved question. Short. Sharp. Incomplete.
> "上个月，我认识了十年的合伙人给我发了条微信：'我想好了。'三个字。那天下午我们的对话只有七句。但它改变了接下来所有事。"

Why it works: Human compulsion to hear the end of a story. The gap between "seven sentences" and "changed everything" is irresistible.

### Banned Openings (INSTANT Reader Loss)

Each one signals "this article has nothing new for you":

- "今天给大家分享..." → You're not sharing. You're writing. Cut the fake humility.
- "随着XX的发展..." → Every AI essay ever written.
- "在当今社会..." → Four characters that mean "I have nothing specific to say."
- "你有没有想过..." + generic question → If the answer is "有" or "没有" with no elaboration, the question is too vague.
- "在这个信息爆炸的时代..." → 2015 called, it wants its opening back.
- "众所周知..." → If everyone knows it, why are you writing about it?
- "相信很多人和我一样..." → Presumptuous AND vague. Double fail.
- Any definition: "什么是XX？XX是指..." → The reader knows roughly what it is. Get to the new stuff.

---

## Ending Rules

- 1-2 sentences maximum. The reader was there — NEVER summarize.
- The ending should change the meaning of something earlier, or leave one thought that won't let go.

### Strong Ending Patterns

**The Boomerang:** Return to the opening with new meaning.
> (Opening was about a friend's career crisis) "后来我问他后悔吗。他说了句话我到现在没想明白：'后悔是后来人才有的资格。'"

**The Uncomfortable Question:** One question the reader will carry with them.
> "所以下次有人跟你说'做自己'的时候，你可以试着问一句：哪个自己？"

**The Quietly Devastating Line:** No exclamation marks. No drama. Just truth.
> "这个世界不缺聪明人。缺的是愿意把聪明用在笨事上的人。"

**The Unfinished Action:** End mid-motion.
> "写到这里，我把那个搁置了半年的项目文件夹打开了。"

### BANNED Endings

- "希望对你有帮助" → Weak. If the article was helpful, this adds nothing.
- "以上就是我的分享" → Newsletter energy. Don't.
- "你觉得呢？欢迎留言讨论" → Generic engagement bait. If you need a question, make it specific.
- "让我们一起..." → Performative solidarity. Readers see through it.
- "综上所述" / any summary → The death knell of good writing.

## The Screenshot Test

The final quality gate: **Does this article contain at least one paragraph good enough to screenshot and send to a friend?**

What makes a screenshot-worthy paragraph:
- A perfectly crystallized insight in 2-3 sentences
- A specific observation that names something the reader has felt but never articulated
- A turn of phrase so precise it becomes quotable

Examples of screenshot-worthy writing:
> "职场最残酷的真相不是'努力没用'。是'努力有用，但有用的程度远远小于你以为的程度'。差距真正拉开的地方，是你下班之后、没人看着的时候，选择做什么。"

> "我们这代人有个毛病：把'知道'当成了'做到'的预支。看了减肥视频觉得自己瘦了三斤，读了理财文章觉得自己赚了三万。"

> "不是世界变了。是世界终于不装了。"

If you finish a 2000-word article and can't find one screenshot-worthy paragraph — the article lacks density. Go back and sharpen.

## De-AI Protocol (4 Levels)

The problem isn't individual words. It's THINKING PATTERNS. AI thinks in categories, lists, and symmetry. Humans think in stories, contradictions, and messy tangents.

### Level 1 — Lexical Purge

Scan and eliminate ALL of these — no exceptions:

**Structural markers (instant AI tells):**
首先、其次、再者、再次、然后、最后、综上所述、总而言之、总之、因此、由此

**AI connector phrases:**
值得一提的是、不禁让人、与此同时、毋庸置疑、显著、基于此、由此可见、
事实上、显然、进一步、此外、另一方面、从而、不可否认、需要强调的是、
在这个...的时代、众所周知、让我们、不言而喻、显而易见、需要注意的是、
毫无疑问、值得关注的是、不难发现、可以说、在某种程度上、越来越多的

**Certainty markers (replace):**
- 证明了 → 初步显示 / 看起来支持
- 表明了 → 我倾向于认为 / 至少说明了
- 毫无疑问 → delete or replace with evidence
- 无可置疑 → delete

**AI四字成语 (instant signal):**
古色古香、跌宕起伏、无微不至、博大精深、源远流长、与时俱进、
如火如荼、方兴未艾、蓬勃发展、日新月异、欣欣向荣、翻天覆地

### Level 2 — Structural De-AI

**Kill parallel structures:**
- ❌ "春天是希望，夏天是热情，秋天是收获，冬天是沉思" (perfect parallel = robot)
- ✅ "春天让人想干大事。夏天让人只想躺着。秋天最危险——它假装一切都成熟了，其实落叶才刚开始。" (asymmetric, the third element detonates)

**Break the 3-point addiction:**
AI ALWAYS lists exactly 3 things. If you have 3 points: cut to 2 or expand to 5. If you must list, VARY depth — one gets 1 sentence, another gets a full paragraph.

**Eliminate guided transitions:**
- ❌ "让我们来看看..." / "接下来我们来聊聊..." / "下面我们来分析..."
- ✅ Jump straight in. Or conversational bridges: "说到这个——" / "但这还不是最狠的" / "等等，有个东西你得先知道"

**Vary paragraph openings:**
If 3+ paragraphs start the same way (noun phrase, or "在..."), rewrite.

### Level 3 — Cognitive Humanization

**Three-Layer Stacking (场景 + 情感 + 感官):**
Not "in a café" — "星巴克靠窗的位子，旁边的人在开电话会议，声音大到他的PPT我都快能背下来了" + emotional layer + sensory detail

**Anti-Formula Reversal:**
[Common belief] → BUT → [unexpected specific detail that undermines it]
> "所有人都说读书改变命运。但我认识读书最多的那个人，去年刚被裁。"

**Five-Senses Anchoring (every 300 words, at least ONE):**
Not generic — surprising sensory details:
> "键盘上的指纹油光" / "外卖塑料袋在门把手上晃荡的声音" / "新买的书翻开时胶水的味道"

**Narrative Direction: Specific → General (NEVER Reverse)**
AI default: conclusion → illustrate. Human default: experience → insight emerges.
> ❌ "社交媒体正在改变消费习惯。例如..." (lecture mode)
> ✅ "我妈上周在抖音买了条裙子。到货那天她举着手机给我看：'这哪是我买的那条？'但她没退。她说'算了，才39块'。三天后她又买了一条。" → Let the insight emerge: 这不是冲动消费，这是"反正便宜"的新消费逻辑

### Level 4 — Final Voice Verification

After completing the draft, answer honestly:

- [ ] Could any paragraph have been written about a COMPLETELY different topic? → Rewrite with specifics.
- [ ] Are there 3+ consecutive paragraphs of similar length? → Vary them.
- [ ] Any sentence with 3+ abstract adjectives? → Replace with ONE concrete detail.
- [ ] Does any section sound like an essay or report? → Rewrite as if texting a smart friend.
- [ ] Are all sentences medium-length? → Add short punches AND one long flowing sentence.
- [ ] Is there at least 1 counterintuitive point? → If not, find one.
- [ ] Can you cut 20% of adjectives/adverbs without losing meaning? → Do it.
- [ ] Read the first sentence of every paragraph in sequence — does it sound like a robot wrote the outline? → Randomize the openings.

## Gotchas — Common Failure Modes

**"The Generic Insight":** "这件事告诉我们一个道理" + something everyone knows. If the insight doesn't surprise YOU, it won't surprise anyone. Dig deeper or cut entirely.

**"The Balanced Bot":** Presenting every side equally without opinion. Take a stance. Acknowledge the counter. Explain why you still disagree. "有人可能会说...他们说得对，但只对了一半" beats "各有各的道理."

**"The Summary Ending":** Restating the article in the conclusion. The reader was there. End with something NEW.

**"The List Disguised as Prose":** Converting bullets to "首先...其次...最后" doesn't make it prose. Real flow is ideas building on each other, surprising each other, contradicting each other.

**"The 300-Word Warm-Up":** Context-setting preamble before the actual point. Cut it. Start at the interesting part. Weave context in AFTER you've hooked them.

**"Emotional Flatline":** Every paragraph at the same intensity. Good articles pulse — tension, release, surprise, reflection, tension again. Chart the emotional intensity; it should look like a heartbeat, not a flatline.

**"The Certainty Disease":** Stating everything as settled fact. "我不确定，但我倾向于..." is more credible than "毫无疑问."

**"The Examples-as-Padding Move":** Three examples making the same point. One sharp example > three generic ones. After the first, ask: does the second add a NEW dimension? If not, cut it.
